Tatung

Singkawang

This tatung tradition is always held in Singkawang City ahead of Cap Go Meh. The celebration of Cap Go Meh in this area takes place very lively with the presence of tatung. In this very lively parade, the tatung will be immune to Sharp Objects, so during the parade tatung will perform demonstrations such as debus.

grebeg syawal

Yogyakarta

The special region of Yogyakarta is already famous for its expertise in maintaining ancestral culture for generations, Grebeg Syawal is one of them. Grebeg Syawal held every 1 Shawwal is a form of gratitude for the presence of the Sultan Eid al-Fitr after a month of fasting in Ramadan. The celebration took place by parading Kakungan and Gunungan Putri composed of vegetables and other crops. This mountain is a symbol of the Sultan's alms to his people. This event is quite unique and builds a sense of togetherness because people are allowed to fight over Gunungan which is believed to bring blessings and prosperity to those who get it. We should be proud of the cultural traditions spread in various regions of the archipelago. These traditions are a form of heritage from the culture and history lived by our ancestors, so it is important for us to learn the values as well as the meaning contained in them.

bakar tongkang

Riau

Ethnic Chinese who live in Bagansiapiapi, Riau always hold a ritual burn tongkak held every July. According to the belief of this ritual has been done by their ancestors with the aim of determined not to return to the place of origin. Another meaning is the memorial service of the sea god Ki Ong Ya and Tai Su Ong who are depicted as two-sided gods.

dugderan

Semarang

Before the month of fasting Semarang city will be crowded with events dugderan. This tradition that has existed since long ago is a marker that the fasting month has come. One of the characteristics of this event is the warak ngendok wine. Warak ngendok is a fictional Star with a goat body, dragon head and golden skin scales.

kebo keboan

Banyuwangi

The ritual, which is held once a year, is always held every 10 Suro or 10 Muharam in Alasmalang village, Singojuruh, Banyuwangi. This ceremony requires several men to dress up as buffalo and make sacrifices to plow the fields. After plowing the fields, these kebos are paraded around the village accompanied by a folk art carnival. This kebo – keboan ritual aims to ask for rain during the dry season.

mekare kare

Bali

The island of Bali also keeps a unique tradition performed by the men in the village of Tenganan Pegringsingan as an offering to the god Indra. In this tradition, the men from the village will perform a war performance using thorny pandanus leaves as weapons and rattan shields to fend off the opponent's attacks. After performing this ritual, all Mekare-kare participants will sit and eat food together while their wounds are treated.
